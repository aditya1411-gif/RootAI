How Weather Affects Your Soil

🌧 Heavy Rain Expected — Day 1 to 3
Your soil moisture is already at 71%. Heavy rain will push it above 85% causing waterlogging and root rot risk. Roots cannot breathe in waterlogged soil and crop health will deteriorate quickly.
Action: Clear all drainage channels today before rain arrives.
🌡 Temperature Rising — Day 5 to 7
Temperature will reach 34 to 35°C after rain clears. Combined with already wet soil this risks root damage and stunted crop growth. High temperature after heavy rain is one of the most damaging combinations for crops.
Action: Apply a mulch layer of 5 to 7 cm after rain stops to reduce soil heat absorption.
💧 Best Time to Apply Fertilizer
Do not apply any fertilizer during rain days 1 to 3. It will wash away into drainage water and waste your money and resources without any benefit to the soil.
Best Window: Day 4 to 5 after rain clears. Apply agricultural lime first then wait 2 days before applying urea fertilizer.
